LASER: Script Execution by Autonomous Agents for On-demand Traffic Simulation

English | 中文
Code

Abstract

Autonomous Driving Systems (ADS) require diverse and safety-critical traffic scenarios for effective training and testing, but the existing data generation methods struggle to provide flexibility and scalability. We propose LASER, a novel framework that leverage large language models (LLMs) to conduct traffic simulations based on natural language inputs. The framework operates in two stages: it first generates scripts from user-provided descriptions and then executes them using autonomous agents in real time. Validated in the CARLA simulator, LASER successfully generates complex, on-demand driving scenarios, significantly improving ADS training and testing data generation.

Framework

To achieve on-demand and interactive traffic simulation, we propose a framework called LASER.
LASER consists of two stages, implemented by two modules respectively, i.e., script writer and LASER-Agent. Unlike the previous learning based methods that conduct generation and simulation simultaneously, our LASER framework first generates scripts that define logic-chained behaviors (LCB) with natural language instructions from the user requirements. It then executes the script by the real-time cooperation of LASER-Agents.

Criteria for reviewing simulations:

      We present the criteria for reviewing simulations to evaluate the effectiveness of script execution.
      

        

            Scene
            Criteria
        

        

            Accident
            The two cars correctly change lanes according to their scripts before colliding with the stopped accident vehicles
        

        

            Ambulance
            As the ambulance approaches, vehicles in the same lane move aside one
              by one to make way, without any collisions.
        

        

            Bus
            The bus changes lanes and stops at the bus station within a ±2.5m range,
              then starts again and changes lanes back.
        

        

          Caught in Pincer
          The sudden lane change by the front car and the acceleration of the rear car caused a collision involving three vehicles.
        

        

          Cut-in
          The car successfully overtakes the front car then slows down.
        

        

          Failed at Start
          The collision occurred when the vehicle started moving as the bus changed lanes.
        

        

          Meet in Mind 1
          Two cars simultaneously changed lanes towards the center, leading to a collision.
        

        

          Meet in Mind 2
          Two cars attempted to overtake at the same time, and a collision occurred when both vehicles changed lanes simultaneously.
        

        

          Merge Alternately
          Vehicles alternately passed through the left lane of the two cars stopped due to the collision.
        

        

          Newbie Lane Change 1
          The vehicle in the front right slowly changed lanes to the left, causing a collision with ego car.
        

        

          Newbie Lane Change 2
          The vehicle in the front right slowly changed lanes to the left, causing a collision with ego car.
        

        

          Reckless Driving
          The reckless car successfully overtakes the first car from the left lane and
            the second car from the right lane.
        

        

          Sudden Jaywalker
          The jaywalker steps out from behind the truck as the ego vehicle approaches.
        

        

          Surrounded
          Four police cars are driving in the front, rear, left, and right directions of the vehicle, respectively.
        

        

          Swerve
          The front car decelerates to a stop. The rear car changes into the ego
            vehicle's lane, nearly hitting the ego vehicle before colliding with the front
            car.
        

        

          Three in Line 1
          The front car decelerates to a stop, while the rear car changes lanes to the
            right before colliding with the front car, and then drives away
        

        

          Three in Line 2
          The vehicle in front slowed down and stopped, and the vehicle behind collided 
            with it and stopped. Ego vehicle intends to turn right to avoid a collision.
